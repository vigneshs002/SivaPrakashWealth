Best insurance plans — overview

How we think about LIC options

The names below are examples only. Product availability, underwriting, and suitability depend on your age, income, health disclosures, and insurer rules at the time of application — we finalise recommendations only after a proper consultation.

By age group
  • 20–30 years: Strong family protection (term-heavy mix) with disciplined savings where budget allows.
  • 30–40 years: Child education and marriage goals, alongside pension / retirement accumulation.
  • 40–50 years: Wealth creation and guaranteed-benefit style plans, balanced with existing cover.
  • 50+ years: Single-premium or limited-pay options where liquidity and health criteria fit.
By annual income (illustrative)
  • Below ₹5 lakh: Long-term endowment-style savings combined with essential term insurance.
  • ₹5–10 lakh: Examples discussed include Jeevan Labh, Jeevan Umang, Bima Shree — subject to eligibility.
  • ₹10–20 lakh: Shorter premium-payment designs such as Jeevan Utsav when they match cash flow.
  • Above ₹20 lakh: Bima Shree, Jeevan Lakshya, and other high sum assured structures after risk review.
Retirement planning

Jeevan Akshay, Jeevan Umang, and Jeevan Shanti are examples we compare for annuity-style or long-term income needs.

Tax-saving angle (80C)

Endowment and whole-life plans with compliant premium levels may support 80C within statutory limits; we map this clearly at proposal stage.

HNWIs & business owners

Higher sum assured through Jeevan Labh, Jeevan Anand, Bima Shree; business owners may also consider shorter-pay or single-premium routes such as Jeevan Utsav when appropriate.

Child education & future planning

Amrit Baal and Jeevan Tarun for education milestones; Jeevan Anand and Jeevan Umang for broader family security — timelines and premiums are aligned to your real goals.
